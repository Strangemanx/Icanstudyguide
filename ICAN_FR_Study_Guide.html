Conceptual Framework & Accounting Policies

The theoretical foundation of IFRS. Understanding this is essential before diving into individual standards.

1 Qualitative Characteristics of Financial Information

CategoryCharacteristics
FundamentalRelevance (materiality, predictive/confirmatory value) and Faithful Representation (complete, neutral, free from error)
EnhancingComparability, Verifiability, Timeliness, Understandability

2 IAS 8 — Accounting Policies, Changes & Errors

IAS 8 requires that when accounting policy changes, it is applied retrospectively — as if the new policy had always been applied.

Limitations of retrospective application (tested in Nov 2025 Q1):

  • Period-specific effect: Impracticable to determine the effect for all prior periods
  • Cumulative effect: Cannot determine the total effect from the beginning of all prior periods
  • Data unavailability: Historical records not maintained in a way that enables adjustment
  • Unreasonable assumptions: New policy cannot be applied without significant unreliable assumptions
  • Excessive cost: Cost of collecting data is prohibitively high
IAS 8 Hierarchy: Changes in accounting policy → retrospective. Changes in accounting estimates → prospective. Prior period errors → retrospective restatement.

3 Rule-Based vs Principle-Based Standards

FeatureRule-BasedPrinciple-Based (IFRS)
NatureDetailed prescriptive rulesConceptual principles requiring judgement
FlexibilityLess flexible, more consistentMore flexible, relies on professional judgement
RiskLoopholes; box-tickingInconsistency between entities; manipulation
ExampleUS GAAP (historically)IFRS
⚠ Nov 2025 Q5: IFRS is principle-based but does contain many rules. The key distinguishing feature is that IFRS rules are derived from underlying conceptual principles.

Topic 02

IAS 16 — Property, Plant & Equipment

PPE is tested in virtually every ICAN FR exam — either as a standalone question or as part of preparing financial statements.

1 Recognition & Initial Measurement

PPE is recognised when it is probable that future economic benefits will flow to the entity and the cost can be reliably measured. Initially measured at cost:

  • Purchase price (less trade discounts)
  • Import duties and non-refundable taxes
  • Costs directly attributable to bringing the asset to the location and condition for use
  • Initial estimate of dismantling/restoration costs (IAS 37 provision)
⚠ Exclude from cost: Administration costs, training costs, abnormal waste during construction, selling/promotion costs.

2 Subsequent Measurement — Cost vs Revaluation Model

ModelCarrying AmountSurplus/Deficit
Cost ModelCost – Accumulated depreciation – ImpairmentN/A
Revaluation ModelFair value at revaluation date – subsequent depreciationSurplus → OCI (Revaluation Reserve); Deficit → P&L (unless reversing prior surplus in OCI)
🎯 Nov 2025 Q1 — Ayoka Foods (Freehold Property Revaluation)

Property cost = ₦112.5m. Revalued to ₦120m on April 1, 2023. Carrying amount before revaluation = ₦97.5m. Revaluation surplus = ₦22.5m → recognised in OCI (Other Comprehensive Income). Deferred tax on the surplus = 25% × ₦22.5m = ₦5.625m → netted in OCI. Net revaluation reserve = ₦16.875m.

3 Depreciation

Depreciation Key Rules
- Depreciate separately significant components (IAS 16.43) - Residual value and useful life reviewed ANNUALLY - Land is NOT depreciated - Depreciation begins when asset is AVAILABLE for use - Depreciation ceases at the earlier of: held for sale date OR derecognition Straight-line: (Cost – Residual Value) / Useful Life Reducing balance: Carrying Amount × Rate % Nov 2025 Q1 — Building: Revalued at ₦120m, land element ₦45m → building = ₦75m Remaining life 20 years → depreciation = ₦75m / 20 = ₦3.75m/year

4 Disposal of PPE

Disposal
Gain/Loss on disposal = Proceeds – Carrying amount at disposal date Carrying amount = Cost – Accumulated depreciation The gain/loss goes to Profit or Loss. Any related revaluation reserve is transferred directly to retained earnings (not recycled through P&L).

Topic 03

IAS 40 — Investment Property

Investment property is land or buildings held to earn rentals or for capital appreciation — not for use in operations.

1 Definition & Classification

Investment property includes:

  • Land held for long-term capital appreciation
  • Land held for currently undetermined use
  • Building owned or leased under finance lease and leased out under operating leases
  • Vacant building held to be leased out under an operating lease
⚠ NOT Investment Property: Property used in production or supply of goods/services (→ IAS 16), or property held for sale in the ordinary course of business (→ IAS 2 / IFRS 5).
🎯 Nov 2025 Q2(c) — Dideolu Group (Three Properties)

Property 1: Rented to a subsidiary — classified under IAS 16 in consolidated accounts (internal use within the group). Depreciate over useful life.
Property 2 & 3: Let to unrelated parties on commercial terms → Investment Property (IAS 40). Use fair value model: gains/losses go to P&L at each year-end.

2 Measurement Models

ModelTreatment
Cost ModelCarried at cost less accumulated depreciation and impairment (same as IAS 16 cost model)
Fair Value Model (preferred by ICAN exams)Carried at fair value each reporting date. Fair value gains/losses → Profit or Loss (NOT OCI — different from IAS 16 revaluation!)
Critical difference from IAS 16: Under IAS 40 fair value model, ALL fair value movements go to P&L. Under IAS 16 revaluation model, surpluses go to OCI. This distinction is tested frequently.

Topic 04

IAS 12 — Income Taxes

Tested in every diet. You must understand current tax, over/under provision, and deferred tax — particularly on revaluations.

1 Current Tax — Under/Over Provision

An over-provision occurs when the actual tax paid is less than the estimate made in the previous year's accounts. It is a credit in the current year's P&L.

An under-provision means the tax paid was more than estimated → charge in current year P&L.

🎯 Nov 2025 Q2(d) — Benson Ltd (Over-provision Treatment)

Provision made in 2023: ₦37.5m. Actual tax paid for 2023: ₦31.5m. Over-provision = ₦6m. This ₦6m credit reduces the income tax charge in the 2024 P&L. The 2024 estimated liability of ₦36.8m is then accrued.

2 Deferred Tax — The Core Concept

Deferred tax arises from temporary differences — differences between the carrying amount of an asset/liability and its tax base.

Deferred Tax Calculation
Temporary Difference = Carrying Amount – Tax Base Deferred Tax Liability (DTL) = Positive temp difference × Tax rate (asset carried above tax base → more tax will be paid in future) Deferred Tax Asset (DTA) = Negative temp difference × Tax rate (liability or loss with future tax benefit) On Revaluation (Nov 2025 Q1): Revaluation surplus = ₦22,500,000 DTL = 25% × ₦22,500,000 = ₦5,625,000 Recognised IN OCI (same place as the revaluation surplus) Net revaluation reserve = ₦22,500,000 – ₦5,625,000 = ₦16,875,000

3 IAS 12 — Offsetting

Deferred tax assets and liabilities may only be offset if the entity has a legally enforceable right to offset current tax assets against current tax liabilities, AND they relate to taxes levied by the same authority.

IAS 33 — Earnings Per Share

EPS is a key investor metric. ICAN tests both basic and diluted EPS, scrip dividends, and rights issues.

1 Basic EPS

Basic EPS
Basic EPS = Profit attributable to ordinary shareholders / Weighted Average Number of Ordinary Shares (WANOS) Profit = Profit after tax – Preference dividends (if any) WANOS: Shares in issue at start × time proportion + New shares × remaining months / 12 (Bonus issue → restate ALL prior periods retroactively — no time weighting) (Rights issue → bonus element needs an adjustment factor)

2 Scrip Dividend — Impact on EPS (Nov 2025 Q2)

A scrip dividend is a bonus issue funded from profits (not cash). Like a bonus issue, it increases shares without new capital — the theoretical effect is a fall in share price and EPS.

🎯 Nov 2025 Q2(b) — Edo Plc Scrip Dividend

Before: 3,000,000 shares at ₦5.00 = ₦15m market cap. EPS = ₦15m / 3,000,000 = ₦5.00

25% scrip dividend: New shares = 25% × ₦15m / ₦2.00 = 1,875,000 shares

After: 4,875,000 shares. New market price = ₦15m / 4,875,000 = ₦3.08. New EPS = ₦11.25m / 4,875,000 = ₦2.31

EPS falls from ₦5.00 to ₦2.31 — numerator reduces (retained earnings used) AND denominator increases.

3 Five Key Features for Computing EPS (Nov 2025 Q2a)

  • Profit attributable to ordinary shareholders (after preference dividends)
  • Weighted average number of ordinary shares (WANOS) — time-apportioned
  • Bonus/scrip issues and share splits → retroactive adjustment of all prior-period EPS
  • Diluted EPS — consider options, convertibles, warrants (potential ordinary shares)
  • Contingently issuable shares — include when virtually certain conditions are met

IFRS 5 — Non-Current Assets Held for Sale & Discontinued Operations

Tested in May 2025 Q5. Crucial for presenting discontinued operations separately from continuing operations.

1 Criteria for Classification as Held for Sale

An asset (or disposal group) is classified as held for sale when ALL of the following are met:

  1. Available for immediate sale in its present condition
  2. Management is committed to a plan to sell
  3. An active programme to locate a buyer is in place
  4. Asset is being marketed at a price reasonable to its fair value
  5. Sale expected to be completed within one year from classification date
  6. It is unlikely the plan will be withdrawn or significantly changed
⚠ Measurement: Once classified as held for sale, measure at the lower of carrying amount or fair value less costs to sell. Stop depreciating.

2 Discontinued Operations — Presentation

A discontinued operation is a component that has been disposed of or is classified as held for sale, and represents:

  • A separate major line of business or geographical area of operations, OR
  • A subsidiary acquired exclusively for resale
Statement of Profit or Loss Presentation (May 2025 Q5b)
Continuing operations: Revenue ₦xxx Cost of sales (₦xxx) Gross profit ₦xxx Operating expenses (₦xxx) Profit from CONTINUING operations ₦xxx Discontinued operations: Profit/(loss) from discontinued ₦xxx ← Single line on face (detailed breakdown in NOTES) Profit for the year ₦xxx Comparative figures MUST be restated to show the prior year discontinued operations separately.
🎯 May 2025 Q5(b) — Abia Nigeria Ltd (Travel Agencies Discontinued)

Travel agencies met IFRS 5 conditions. 2024: Loss from discontinued = (₦24,000,000). Continuing: Internet ₦24,000 + Car hire ₦2,400 = ₦26,400,000. Comparative 2023 restated: Travel agencies profit = ₦9,000,000 shown separately.

IFRS 15 — Revenue from Contracts with Customers

IFRS 15 replaced IAS 18 and IAS 11. It uses a single 5-step model for all revenue recognition — tested in May 2025 Q6.

1 The 5-Step Model

StepWhat You Do
Step 1Identify the contract(s) with a customer — must be approved, rights identifiable, commercial substance, probable collection
Step 2Identify the performance obligations — each distinct good or service is a separate obligation
Step 3Determine the transaction price — fixed or variable, time value of money, non-cash consideration
Step 4Allocate the transaction price to each performance obligation based on standalone selling prices
Step 5Recognise revenue when (or as) each performance obligation is satisfied (control transferred)

2 Distinct Goods and Services (Step 2)

A good or service is distinct if BOTH of the following are met:

  • The customer can benefit from it on its own or with other readily available resources
  • The entity's promise to transfer it is separately identifiable from other promises in the contract
Core Principle of IFRS 15: Recognise revenue to depict the transfer of goods or services to customers in an amount that reflects the consideration the entity expects to be entitled to in exchange for those goods or services.

3 Combining Contracts (Step 1 — Special Rule)

Two or more contracts may be combined and treated as one if:

  • Negotiated as a package with a single commercial objective, OR
  • Consideration in one contract depends on price/performance of the other, OR
  • Goods/services together form a single performance obligation

Topic 08

IAS 37 — Provisions, Contingent Liabilities & Assets

Tested in May 2025 Q7. Three distinct items with very different recognition and disclosure treatment.

1 Provisions — Recognition

Recognise a provision when ALL THREE criteria are met:

  1. Entity has a present obligation (legal or constructive) as a result of a past event
  2. It is probable (more likely than not) that an outflow of economic benefits will be required
  3. A reliable estimate can be made of the amount
Measurement: Best estimate of expenditure required to settle. For single obligations — most likely outcome. For large populations — expected value (probability-weighted).

2 Contingent Liabilities

A contingent liability is either:

  • A possible obligation (existence uncertain, depends on future events), OR
  • A present obligation that fails the provision criteria (outflow not probable or amount not reliably estimated)

Treatment: Never recognised in financial statements. Disclosed in notes unless the possibility of outflow is remote.

3 Contingent Assets

A possible asset whose existence depends on future uncertain events.

Treatment: Never recognised. Disclosed in notes only if an inflow is probable (not just possible).

Group Accounts — Consolidation (IFRS 3 & IFRS 10)

The biggest topic in FR. Tested as compulsory or Section B in almost every diet — Nov 2025 Q4, May 2025 Q3.

1 Goodwill on Acquisition (IFRS 3)

Goodwill Calculation (Full Goodwill / Fair Value Method)
Goodwill = Consideration transferred + Non-controlling interest (at fair value or % of net assets) – Fair value of net assets acquired at acquisition date Consideration = Cash paid + Deferred consideration (PV) + Contingent consideration (FV at acquisition) Note on contingent consideration: - Remeasured at each reporting date (financial liability) - Changes in fair value → P&L (not goodwill) - Nov 2025 Q4: Contingent consideration fell ₦2.7m → ₦2.25m → ₦450,000 GAIN in P&L Negative goodwill (bargain purchase): - Recognised immediately in P&L (IFRS 3) - NOT allocated to NCI - Nov 2025 Q4: Negative goodwill of ₦5.1m → credit to P&L in admin expenses

2 Non-Controlling Interest (NCI)

NCI Calculation
Method 1 — Fair value (full goodwill): NCI = Fair value of NCI at acquisition + NCI share of post-acquisition reserves – NCI share of goodwill impairment Method 2 — Proportionate share: NCI = NCI % × Net assets at acquisition date + NCI % × post-acquisition reserves Nov 2025 Q4 — Dimeji Ltd: NCI at acquisition (fair value): 9,000 × 2 shares × 25% × ₦1.20 = ₦5,400,000 Post-acquisition adjusted loss (6 months): ₦4.5m × 25% = ₦(1,125,000) NCI at year-end: ₦4,275,000

3 Key Consolidation Adjustments

  • Intra-group sales: Eliminate in full from revenue and cost of sales
  • Unrealised profit (URP) in inventory: Remove from group inventory and consolidated retained earnings
  • Fair value adjustments: Adjust subsidiary's net assets at acquisition and depreciate extra FV over remaining life
  • Intra-group loans: Eliminate from both payables and receivables
  • Deferred consideration: Unwind the discount → finance cost in P&L (May 2025 Q3)
🎯 May 2025 Q3 — Mana/Bakus Unrealised Profit

Mana bought goods from Bakus. Profit on intra-group sale = ₦24m. 1/3 of goods still in Mana's inventory at year-end. URP = 1/3 × ₦24m = ₦8m. Reduce group inventory by ₦8m; reduce group retained earnings by ₦8m (buyer holds goods so charge to Mana's retained earnings).

Sustainability Reporting & ISSB (IFRS S1 & S2)

A new and growing area in the ICAN syllabus. Tested in Nov 2025 Q6. Expected to increase in future diets.

1 What is Sustainability Reporting?

Sustainability reporting is the practice of publicly disclosing a company's performance across Environmental, Social and Governance (ESG) factors to drive accountability and transparency.

Key features (Nov 2025 Q6a):

  • Forward-looking: Includes scenario analysis, forecasts and strategy alignment
  • Materiality-driven: Focuses on ESG issues that could affect enterprise value
  • Quantitative and qualitative: KPIs (e.g. emissions, water use) plus narratives
  • Integrated with financial reporting: Aligned with IFRS S1 and S2
  • Standardised by GRI and IFRS
  • Value chain oriented: Looks beyond immediate operations

2 Objectives and Benefits of Sustainability Reporting

  • Transparency and accountability on ESG impacts
  • Informed decision-making — investors assess long-term risks
  • Regulatory compliance — meeting mandatory ESG requirements
  • Enhancing reputation and attracting ESG-focused investors
  • Attracting capital — growing investor interest in ESG
  • Risk identification — climate change, supply chain ESG risks
  • Performance measurement — track progress against ESG targets

3 ISSB — International Sustainability Standards Board

The ISSB was created by IFRS Foundation to develop global sustainability disclosure standards for capital markets:

  • IFRS S1 — General Requirements for Disclosure of Sustainability-related Financial Information
  • IFRS S2 — Climate-related Disclosures

Importance of ISSB (Nov 2025 Q6c):

  • Provides a single authoritative source of sustainability disclosure standards for capital markets
  • Reduces reporting burden on multinationals by aligning standards globally
  • Promotes trust, accountability and efficiency in ESG data
  • Enables integration of sustainability into financial decision-making
  • Addresses fragmentation and lack of comparability in sustainability disclosures
IFRS S1 vs S2: S1 is the general framework for all sustainability-related disclosures. S2 specifically addresses climate risk — physical risks (floods, droughts) and transition risks (policy changes, technology shifts).
